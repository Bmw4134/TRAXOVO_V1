Good vs Bad AI Communication Examples

The key to AI success is being specific, detailed, and providing context.

‚ùå Bad Example
"Generate a report for our equipment"
Too vague - what kind of report? Which equipment? What time period? What format?
‚úÖ Good Example
"Generate a monthly utilization report for all earthwork equipment (excavators, bulldozers, graders) from March 1-31, 2025. Include: operating hours, fuel consumption, maintenance costs, and driver assignments. Format as Excel with charts for executive presentation."
Specific timeframe, equipment types, required metrics, and output format clearly defined.
‚ùå Bad Example
"Fix the attendance system"
No details about what's broken, expected behavior, or scope of the fix needed.
‚úÖ Good Example
"The attendance matrix is not displaying driver hours for May 16-19, 2025. It should show a grid with employee IDs on rows, dates on columns, and hours worked in each cell. The CSV files are uploaded in attached_assets folder. Need it to automatically detect late starts (after 7 AM) and early ends (before 4 PM)."
Identifies specific problem, expected behavior, data source, and additional requirements.

AI Communication Best Practices

1
Be Extremely Specific
Instead of "show me data," say "show me equipment utilization data for excavators E001-E025 from March 1-15, 2025, with daily breakdowns."
2
Provide Complete Context
Include file locations, date ranges, expected formats, and any business rules or constraints that apply.
3
Define Success Criteria
Clearly state what the end result should look like, how it should behave, and what metrics indicate success.
4
Use TRAXOVO Standards
Reference authentic GAUGE data, RAGLE billing files, and existing system terminology for consistency.
5
Request Testing Instructions
Ask for specific steps to verify the solution works correctly with your actual data.
6
Iterate and Refine
Build on previous requests with "Additionally, modify the previous solution to..." for incremental improvements.
